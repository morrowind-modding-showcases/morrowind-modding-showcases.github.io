A History of the
Morrowind Modathons

The original Modathon Modding Competition, the Morrowind Modathons have been a proud annual tradition since 2015. Hosted each year between May 1st and June 2nd, the Modathons are a celebration, a modding extravaganza to commemorate Morrowind's anniversary, open to any and all!

Organized and hosted by the community, the Morrowind Modathons have seen thousands of mod submissions by hundreds of mod authors over the years, covering every category imaginable, bringing players and modders together to build upon, improve, and celebrate this amazing game and community, a community that has only continued to grow with the passing of time!

Today there are many other Modathons for many other games, but the Morrowind Modathons were the first, and remain a blazing beacon of the spirit and creativity of this amazing modding community!

Look upon our works, ye mighty, and REJOICE! For see what we can accomplish, what we can build, when we collaborate and work together, striving forward as a community! The best games never die and the future is ours, all of us, to shape - and that future is very bright indeed!
Long live Morrowind and long live the Morrowind Modding Community!

CHAPTER 01

I. Origins

The Morrowind Modathons, like most community-focused projects from the mid-2010s, can trace their origins to the Future of Morrowind thread, posted by Melchior Dahrk on the Bethesda Forums in July of 2014.

The thread sought to address the community's biggest issues at that time, a community that was actively in decline. The mid-2010s were arguably the darkest period of Morrowind's modding history - two of the community's largest mod hosting websites, Planet Elder Scrolls and Elric Melnibone in Morrowind, had both gone offline in 2012, resulting in the loss of countless mods and two of the community's biggest platforms.

The Morrowind modding scene of 2014 was one in dire straits; declining new mod releases, a lack of feedback for mods that were released, and low modder retention resulting in newer modders often moving on to the greener pastures of more recent games.

The Future of Morrowind thread sought to bring the community together to address these issues, and through community discussion, a number of new community-focused projects were born, like the Morrowind Modding Wiki and Morrowind Modding Showcases.

While not a direct result of the Future of Morrowind thread, the creation of the Morrowind Modding Showcases channel would lead to the creation of a number of other projects aimed at increasing community engagement, including the launch of Morrowind Modding Interviews, a podcast video series, in the Autumn of 2014.

Also created during that period, though not yet unveiled to the public, was the first conception of the Morrowind Modathons. In October of 2014, the subject of a Morrowind modding competition was first broached between Melchior Dahrk and Darkelfguy, ultimately deciding on the name "The Morrowind Modathon". The competition was originally planned to be held in December of 2014, but due to other real-life constraints, the event was shelved for a later date.

CHAPTER 02

II. The Inaugural Competition - The 2015 Morrowind May Modathon Month

In April of 2015, the wider Elder Scrolls Modding Community was roiled by scandal over Bethesda's plans to introduce paid mods for The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim. The response was overwhelmingly negative, and resulted in mass review bombing of all of Bethesda's games.

With the newest game in The Elder Scrolls franchise facing such a massive backlash, the idea of the Morrowind Modathon was revived as a pro-community event to bring modders together in a positive light, in stark contrast to all of the negativity surrounding the paid mods fiasco.

The event was given its full formal name "The Morrowind May Modathon Month Modding Competition" and set to begin on May 1st, 2015 - the 13th anniversary of the release of The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind.

The first competition only ran until June 1st, and saw the release of 15 participating mods. A small figure by today's standards, but already that small figure marked it as one of the most successful modding competitions in Morrowind modding history.

Many other modding competitions had been attempted for Morrowind, of course. Most notable were a series of small competitions hosted by Great House Fliggerty. But none ever surpassed a dozen mods, and so, with 15 releases spread across two platforms (Nexus Mods and Great House Fliggerty), the Modathon had already achieved one record, and was set to begin a new proud annual tradition for the Morrowind Modding Community.

To cap off the event, Nix-Hound Replacer by PeterBitt and Pherim won the inaugural Morrowind Modathon, followed by the quest mod Love in the Time of Daedra by HangHimHigher and the player home Deepscorn by Istreddify in the runner-up positions.

CHAPTER 03

III. Before the Age of Achievements (2016-2017)

The second year of the Morrowind Modathons proved to be much more successful than the first, fueled in part by greater visibility and promotions by the Morrowind Modding Showcases YouTube channel. While much the same as the first Modathon in terms of criteria, the competition ended up with nearly double the submissions with 29 mods submitted by 21 mod authors, making it, at that time, the largest modding competition in Elder Scrolls modding history.

2016 was also the first year where MWSE mods played a significant role in the number of submissions for the Modathon, two years before the evolution of MWSE lua.

While by and large a major success, the 2016 Modathon was not without its controversies. Originally intended to be a judged event, with a panel of judges to go through the modding results, the unexpected doubling of submissions proved a headache for getting timely scores in, and the judging portion of the event was ultimately abandoned in favor of a forum-hosted popular vote instead.

This proved somewhat problematic, for while the results of the first Modathon in 2015 had also been determined by popular vote, the 2016 outcome was plagued with accusations of vote rigging. At the heart of the issue was Bal Isra Rising, a player stronghold mod by Dagoth Agahnim. Agahnim was a long-time member of the modding community, she had been active since the late 2000s and had released her Bal Isra Rising after years of development for the Modathon, but in a decidedly buggy state.

Despite the buggy release of Bal Isra Rising, and despite a decided lack of endorsements on the Nexus, the mod ultimately won the popular vote over other entries that had significantly higher endorsement totals on the Nexus, such as the runner-ups Scamp Replacer by PeterBitt and Pherim and Belial - A Dismal Daedric Companion by HangHimHigher.

In the end, the result held, but the forum-based popular voting method was abandoned in subsequent Modathons.

2017

The third Morrowind Modathon, hosted in 2017, saw a return to a calmer set of results, matching the overall number of submissions from 2016, but without any major controversy. In total, 29 mods were submitted for that event by some 25 mod authors.

Notably, this would be the first Modathon for many of the community's legends, with celebrated mod authors like Greatness7, Remiros, Mort, AliceL93, and Lucevar taking part for the first time, alongside many other community veterans that had joined the year before, like Danae, Abot, and PikachunoTM. The Modathon was becoming one of the community's biggest annual events.

Avoiding a forum vote, the winners of the 2017 Modathon were determined by a panel of judges, with the quest mod Immersive Madness by Rot winning first place, ahead of the gameplay mod The Morrowind Randomizer by Mort and Greatness7, and the item mod The Royal Game of Ur by Lord Berandas.

CHAPTER 04

IV. 2018 - The Introduction of Achievements

By all accounts, the 2018 Modathon was set to follow closely in the footsteps of the Modathons that had come before, with no major changes planned. Initial discussions between Melchior Dahrk and Darkelfguy regarding the 2018 Modathon held no mention of anything different, until, somewhat randomly, Darkelfguy suggested the idea of adding achievement badges to the competition a mere two weeks before the competition was set to begin, an attempt to "game-ify" the modding process.

Melchior Dahrk immediately jumped on this idea, quickly coming up with a batch of achievement designs and pretty soon there were 59 achievement badges ready to be awarded to modders for creating mods.

These achievements didn't quite have the standardization of the achievements of later Modathons, there was no visual distinction between category, metric, and hidden achievements. But there were still copper, silver, and gold achievements, each of which had names associated with particular Morrowind creatures or characters, along with a mastery featuring an engraving of the character in question.

Many of these references still exist in the achievement names today, though the masteries have long since been removed.

By sheer serendipity, the introduction of achievements for the 2018 Morrowind Modathon coincided with the advent of lua scripting for MWSE, making all new types of mods possible for the very first time in the game's history.

The result was an explosion in growth, modders quickly became obsessed with unlocking as many achievements as they could, and the exciting new potentials of MWSE lua encouraged modders to add all sorts of new features to the then 16 year old game.

89 mods were submitted for the 2018 Modathon, nearly three times the number of mods submitted the year before, and many of them utilized the new capabilities of MWSE lua. The gameplay mods category came to dominate the event, with 28 total submissions, nearly the total of all submissions for 2017, led by now-familiar names such as NullCascade, Merlord, Mort, and Greatness7.

The surge in submissions wasn't just limited to gameplay mods though, nearly every category saw a major burst of activity. The addition of achievements had unlocked (pardon the pun) an as of yet untapped reservoir of enthusiasm among mod authors, and though it would lead to new problems further down the road, for now it would seem the Modathons had well and truly found their hook in the community.

The 2018 Modathon concluded with a bang, an overall massive increase in, not just the quantity of the mods, but the quality as well, leading to an unprecedented tie between first, second, and third place, with some 13 mods tied in total.

First place was a tie between the items, quests, and dungeon mod Mage Robes by Melchior Dahrk, and the gameplay mod Poison Crafting by Greatness7.

But those two mods were closely followed by a three-way tie for second place between the immersion mod Frostwind - A Camping Survival Mod by Merlord (the predecessor to 2020's AshFall), the gameplay mod Controlled Consumption by NullCascade, and the gameplay mod Happy Harvesting also by NullCascade.

The remaining 8 mods tying for third place were Draugr Diversity by Grond1911 (Creatures & NPCs), Remiros' Tomb Expansion by Remiros (Dungeon Mods and the predecessor to OAAB - Tombs and Towers), Easy Escort by NullCascade (Gameplay Mods), Morrowind Resources Scanner by Abot (Utilities and Resources), Putting Power in Willpower by R-Zero (Gameplay Mods), Diligent Defenders by NullCascade (Gameplay Mods), Vaba-Amus Companion Character by TheDrunkenMudcrab (Companion Mods), and Expeditious Exit by NullCascade (Gameplay Mods).

CHAPTER 05

V. A Seasonal Misadventure - The Short-Lived Achievements Expansion

The massive success of achievement badges in the 2018 Modathon led Darkelfguy to the, in retrospect, very foolish idea that achievements should be a year-round thing, with unique seasonal achievements for each part of the year.

The plan was to award achievement badges to different categories of mods during different seasons, starting with the 2018 Summer of Content, with achievement badges focused on quest, dungeon, town and city mods, and other new content additions to the game.

Melchior Dahrk, for whatever reason, went along with this foolhardy plan, designing some 42 new achievement badges alongside new promo posters and banners. None of the achievements were ever issued to any mods.

It soon became evident that, with no means to automatically send out achievement unlocks, and with no means of tracking participating mods for long periods of time, that it was simply too much work to try and do achievements year-round.

Again, this should have been obvious, present-day Darkelfguy spends 120-150 hours each year just checking, documenting and sending out achievements, and that's with years of supplemental documents and spreadsheets designed to make tracking and sending achievements out easier - and it still takes that long to cover mods for a SINGLE month.

No, Todd, it DOESN'T JUST WORK! That's the WHOLE problem TODD! Achievement badges have to be sent out manually! IT DOESN'T JUST WORK!

Doing achievements year-round would've been a full-time job, and so the plans for Seasonal Achievement badges were promptly scrapped, a now largely forgotten misadventure in the history of the Modathon.

That failure, however, didn't change the fact that achievements, at least for the Modathon itself, were here to stay.

CHAPTER 06

VI. Growing Pains (2019-2020)

If the surge in new mod releases for the 2018 Modathon was a sign of the popularity of achievement badges, the absolute torrent of submissions for the 2019 Modathon was a sledgehammer.

The 2018 Modathon had already set the record for biggest modding competition for an Elder Scrolls game, but 2019 was set to blow it out of the water. Ultimately, 241 new Morrowind mods were submitted for the 2019 Modathon by some 81 different mod authors, well over twice the 2018 haul, and an order of magnitude more than the 2017 Modathon.

The event saw releases by a variety of the community's legendary old guard, like Stuporstar, Hrnchamd, and Trainwiz, as well as countless releases by some of the community's newest members, like Tel Shadow, Ruffin Vangarr, Ysfya, and Kindi - modders that would go on to become a regular presence in the community for years to come.

The massive influx of new mods wasn't, however, without problems, and indeed, issues cropped up right away. The 2019 Modathon was the first competition year to have issues with "mod spam" - low-effort submissions released at volume all at once, flooding the front page and burying other mods.

While this would be the first year with this particular issue, it would not be the last. The issue would persist until 2022, with different mod authors, at different times, releasing torrents of mod spam, causing considerable ire from their fellow modders whose works were subsequently buried from the public eye.

The problem stemmed not from malice or ill will, but from an overly enthusiastic fixation on achievement badges - the allure of the shiny pngs proved to be the source of an unexpected issue for a community that just 5 years earlier was experiencing a drought of new mod releases.

Where once mods could enjoy their time in the sun on the front page, getting amble views and downloads from interested players, now they might be shoved off the front page within mere hours, and not by work that had taken time and effort to make, but quick minor edits that took at most a minute or two. There was understandable consternation by hard-working modders who felt short-changed by the flood.

Initially, Darkelfguy sought to address the problem by speaking with the mod authors in question, erring for diplomacy to resolve the issue, and that worked. But each year, the issue would crop up again, by a different mod author. The source, it seemed, was the Army of One achievement badge, awarded to the "Most Prolific" mod author in each Modathon year. Consequently it was determined that the badge, along with the "Nine Divines" achievement, should be axed, but they weren't officially removed from the achievement rosters until 2023, and thus the issues of mod spam persisted through to 2022.

Notably, the "mod spam" releases were never officially counted as part of the Modathon mod submission lists, and in 2023, a rule was added to the competition that no more than 5 submissions by any one mod author would be accepted within a 24-hour period, to further discourage mass-mod releases.

Spam entries weren't the only new issue introduced in 2019, for even without the "mod spam", there were 241 legitimate entries in 2019, and the Modathon was still a judge-based competition. No one had anticipated how large the spike of new releases would be, and the reality soon became clear that no judge panel would be able to review 241 mods in any reasonable time period.

Given the controversy surrounding the 2016 results when a forum vote had been held, and the added complications that the Modathon was a multi-site competition, with mods posted to Great House Fliggerty and The Assimilation Lab, in addition to Nexus Mods, no easy method of determining a winner was found.

Later, it was determined that Graphic Herbalism - MWSE and OpenMW by Stuporstar and Greatness7 had won the competition. But other notable mods included some of the most popular mods of 2019, like Perfect Placement by Hrnchamd, Remiros' Groundcover, Vvardenfell and Solstheim Worldspace Remaster by the VSW team, Merlord's Character Backgrounds by Merlord, Pincushion by Hrnchamd, and far too many more to name.

Even with the growing pains, the 2019 Modathon had been an unquestionable success.

2020

But of course, the troubles that ailed the 2019 Modathon persisted into 2020, as no immediate changes had been made to the judging criteria. The original supposition had been that, perhaps 2019 had peaked, and 2020 would be more manageable. It was not, and 229 new mods were submitted by some 75 mod authors.

The year was another notable one for gameplay mods, and particularly MWSE mods, with major new gameplay features like The Guar Whisperer and AURA, as well as modern lua adaptions of classic Morrowind mods like Let There Be Darkness (an adaption of True Lights and Darkness), and major landscape overhauls like Sheogorad - Groves of the North and Heart of the Velothi - Gnisis.

Much like 2019, the year saw the return of some old timers, with Von Djangos and Tenner publishing new mods, as well as the debut of other prolific authors that would go on to be a major force in the community like Necrolesian, Vegetto, Tewlwolow, and OperatorJack.

The 2020 Modathon would also see the first dungeon mod release by then-unknown Dungeon Daddy Seelof, the start of a legacy of dungeon overhauls that would cement Seelof as one of the best dungeon modders in the community.

But like the 2019 Modathon, when it came time to select a winner, the sheer volume of mods proved to be too much for a judge-based panel to solve. A winner was declared long after the fact with Mistify by Melchior Dahrk, but to be sure, there were many mods released that year that were worthy of that title.

The troubles with finding a better solution to selecting Modathon winners were rooted in the event's multi-site nature, with mod releases split between Nexus Mods, Great House Fliggerty, and The Assimilation Lab. These early days of achievement badges even encouraged modders to upload on other sites, with the "What's a Nexus?" achievement.

But less than 0.4% of mods had been uploaded to other platforms in 2020, and it was time, finally, to abandon the premise of a multi-site competition format and focus solely on one platform, Nexus Mods. With the new focus, winners could be more easily, and fairly, decided, in this modern age of mass mod releases.

CHAPTER 07

VII. Rise of the Championships (2021)

In 2021, the Morrowind Modathon saw its first major change since the addition of achievements in 2018, with new changes to the rules and criteria of the competition, and a new set of championship badges designed for the most popular mods in each of the major modding categories for Morrowind.

The championships were created, in part as a solution to the judging woes, as the most endorsed mod in each category by the end of the competition's tracking period would automatically be crowned champion, and in part also a solution to the competition's fairness.

By dividing winners by category, mod authors who specialized in less popular categories, like player homes or dungeon mods, would still have a chance to win, as they were no longer directly competing with the more popular categories like gameplay and graphics mods.

The changes came not a moment too soon, for 2021 was another big year for the Modathon, with 236 new mod released by some 86 individual mod authors, covering every category, which now, for the first time, all had an equal chance of winning.

The average quality of the mods for the 2021 Modathon was staggering, and it was the first Modathon to feature mods utilizing the, then, newly released OAAB Data asset repository, which gave modders a massively expanded library of standardized assets to use in the creation of content mods.

It was also another major year for MWSE mods, with many taking the top spots in the final championship standings. Most notable was Character Sound Overhaul by Anumaril21, which became the most downloaded and most endorsed mod of the 2021 Modathon. To this day, it remains in the top 30 of both the most downloaded and most endorsed Modathon mods ever released.

Other notable highlights included plenty of content mods, and most notably a number of location overhauls, such as; The Corprusarium Experience by Duo Dinamico, featuring an overhaul of the Corprus Bowels of Tel Fyr, a re-master and re-release of the 2018 Modathon mod The Stone Halls of Solstheim by Naufragous77, overhauling some of the Nordic barrows of Solstheim, an overhaul of the Redoran Council Hall cleverly named "Redoran Council Overhall" by Ysfya, Lucevar and Vidi_Aquam, and an extensive overhaul of the game's Dunmer Strongholds by Dallara1000.

There were new gameplay mods expanding the game's stealth mechanics by Mort, an NPC AI overhaul by Archimag, a beautiful new creature replacer by the 2015 Modathon winner PeterBitt, a new climbing skill mod, a giant forested landscape overhaul of the Bitter Coast by Melchior Dahrk (Verticality!), and far, far more besides.

As the third year in a row to feature 200+ mod submissions, it would seem the community had well and truly entered a new era of Morrowind modding, a renaissance that the Modathons only served to highlight further.

CHAPTER 08

VIII. 2022 - Morrowind's 20th Anniversary Celebration

The dawn of Morrowind's 20th year saw a community, not struggling, but thriving, a modding scene that was growing, a renewed enthusiasm that lay in stark contrast to the anniversaries that had come before. Morrowind's 10th birthday had been mired by the closing of Morrowind's two biggest mod hosting platforms. The 15th anniversary had seen a community much reduced in scale from the days of its golden age.

But that golden age had returned again, and so on the day of Morrowind's 20th anniversary, it came to be that the Morrowind Modathon would experience its biggest competition yet, a modding extravaganza like no other that had come before or has come since.

Nearly 300 new Morrowind mods were released as part of the Modathon during the month of May, by 115 individual mod authors. An unparalleled amount for the competition, a record that stands to this day. Dozens of mods were released in nearly every category, a smorgasbord of new content and features, giving Morrowind the 20th anniversary it so very rightfully deserved for such a landmark milestone.

Amongst the plethora of new mod releases were all sorts of new features, like a functional necromancy system in Morrowind with NecroCraft, combat overhauls with PvP and Combat Enhanced, and new character generation features with mtrCultures.

There were remasters of classic Morrowind quest mods, with both a modernization for the legendary Brother Junipers Twin Lamps faction mod, originally released in 2004, and Erengard Mines, originally released in 2003, one of the first player-business management mods for Morrowind.

There were, not one, but two overhauls and expansions for the city of Balmora, with Balmora Waterworks and Amazing Balmora Expansion, the latter of which has a real claim to the title of largest Balmora expansion.

Dungeon modders had their fill, for on the 20th anniversary, Morrowind had two separate overhauls of the game's shipwrecks with OAAB Shipwrecks and Get Wrecked, the first two overhaul mods ever made for Morrowind's oft-forgotten sunken locations.

The Great Hive of Baan Binif and The Lonely Towers of Bal'laku reshaped much of the northern Ashlands with scenic, desolate landmarks to draw the eye.

And GrumblingVomit's Repopulated Morrowind became one of the most popular dynamic NPC mods for Morrowind, rivaling older classics like Morrowind Comes Alive.

Alongside all of that, Morrowind received a massive new faction mod, the Astrologian's Guild, that called back to Morrowind's first golden days, when big new faction and quest mods were all the rave, providing a massive array of new adventures to go on!

It wasn't all exciting new mod releases though, for the 2022 Modathon also saw new additions to the achievements format. Namely the creation of new weekly themed challenges, with associated badges, and a formal distinction of the metric and category achievements into their own unique visual styles.

The themed challenges, optional weekly writing prompts that modders could use to generate ideas and unlock new achievements, saw a fair number of entries in their first year, and have since become a staple of not just the Morrowind Modathon, but every Modathon for every game. Baldur's Gate 3, the Fallout Modathon, the GG Mods Modathon - while being broader competitions than any one theme, they all feature the optional weekly themed challenge format.

In the case of the 2022 Modathon, most of the themed entries were quest mods, with the most popular themes being Blood & Faith and Vanity & Alteration, with some 20% of modders submitting mods under one of the weekly themes, though few managed to complete them all, with the final challenge met only by a mere 2.6% of the competition's modders.

While the success of the themed challenges was far from certain at that point, what was abundantly clear was that the Morrowind Modding Community, on Morrowind's 20th anniversary, had a new lease on life. The sky was limitless, the mods plentiful, and the future looked as golden as the past!

CHAPTER 09

IX. An Explosion of Content, A Decline in Gameplay (2023 and 2024)

Since the advent of MWSE Lua in 2018, gameplay mods had come to dominate the Morrowind Modathons, with dozens of submissions each year, often bordering on the 50-75 range in that one category alone.

But the 2023 and 2024 Morrowind Modathons would prove to be more content driven competitions, dominated more by quests and dungeons, player homes and item mods, and a generally higher percentage of graphics replacers, than many of the more recent Modathons had been.

The decline in gameplay submissions, albeit still the most popular category in terms of new mods, would also coincide with a decline in overall mod submissions, and the 2023 Morrowind Modathon, with 185 new mods released by some 90 individual modders, was a noticeable decline from the year's previous mad frenzy.

That decline of overall submissions was noticeably not reflective of quality, for indeed, it could be argued that the 2023 Morrowind Modathon had one of the highest average standards for mod quality out of any competition hosted by this point in the community. There were so many gems, covering a variety of fantastic new features for the game!

Perhaps the biggest headliner of the event was a massive quest mod by none other than Douglas Goodall, one of Morrowind's original game developers, fleshing out the questing content for low level characters in the game's starting areas.

That mod actually tied with another large quest mod, Temple Master by Melchior Dahrk, which was actually submitted, not just for the Modathon, but was a double-submission, submitted for the 2023 Morrowind Temple Modding Competition (hosted by Teirdalin) as well, which it also won. The two competitions briefly overlapped, allowing Melchior to complete a rare cheeky double victory.

Other notable questing adventures from 2023 included Ius the Animal God, a new questline for one of Tamriel's forgotten deities, The Vanishing Ash-Chirps, one of the most atmospheric  adventures from the competition, and The Mysterious Affair of Sara Shenk, a fun little expansion on Shenk's Shovel with a truly enjoyable story twist.

Landscape overhauls and new dungeon mods were also plentiful, with the standouts being The Mountain of Fear, an overhaul of Mount Kand in vanilla Morrowind, the incomplete but gorgeous Secret of Vilmuhn (that sadly remains unfinished), and dungeon overhauls with BDI - Nchuleft, Vassamsi Island, and Skeeve Den Cult of Namira.

Of course, the biggest and certainly most popular dungeon addition was Caldera Priory by the champion Dungeon Daddy Seelof, a massive sprawling intricately detailed adventure into the depths and catacombs of untold horrors. It remains, arguably, one of the biggest dungeon mods ever made for Morrowind.

Gameplay mods had their highs as well, even with the lower turnout, with standouts Ultimate Fishing, a fully functional fishing mini-game for Morrowind, and Wonders of Water, adding new depths (and terrors) to Morrowind's seas. And in graphics and audio, Impact Sounds and Window to Aetherius provided new stimulating sensations to make the world of Tamriel come alive!

2024

Fast forwarding a year, 2024 was much the same as 2023, with a lower mod total compared to the frantic pace that had greeted Morrowind's 20th anniversary, and like 2023, nearly all of that drop in new mod submissions was focused on largely one category - namely gameplay mods.

In total, 173 new mods were released for the 2024 Morrowind Modathon by 89 individual mod authors, a slight dip from the year before, and over 120 less than the highs of 2022. But like 2023, they were mostly content mods, and the gameplay mods category in particular fell to its lowest level since 2017, with only 24 mods submitted.

The drop in new gameplay mods can, most likely, be attributed to a decline in new MWSE lua mods in 2024. During the Modathon's first few years of achievement badges, gameplay mods had dominated the scene, with MWSE lua being the chief motivating force. But by 2024, after 6 years of MWSE lua development, most of the low-hanging features and gameplay implementations had already been made. And while it was too early for it to have an impact on the 2024 Modathon, OpenMW had finally adopted lua, and there would soon be another tsunami of gameplay mods on the horizon.

But aside from mods, the 2024 Morrowind Modathon also saw a major change in achievement designs, embracing a more stargazing theme. Inspired by the 2024 total eclipse, the achievement badges for the Modathon featured the moons of Nirn in eclipse, with each of the weekly themed challenges featuring the moons in a different phase. A masterful bit of graphical design by Melchior Dahrk!

These new designs were accompanied by an expansion of achievement badges, with the achievements for completing the weekly challenge themes given a little more weight, rewarding modders who dared embrace their creativity with yet more shiny cheevos!

And there were a number of intriguing and exciting mods that took advantage of those themes, from Red Wisdom - An Ashlander Prophecy, a quest mod which ended up being the most popular and most downloaded mod of the competition, to new landmasses with Baal Duun - Cursed Isle of the Four Corners, to atmospheric new dungeon delves like The Woe of Indalen Tomb and Dwemer Sky Fortress.

Quite a few smaller quest mods were released for those themes as well, zany and fun little adventures like Mudcrabmancer, Veil of the Forgotten, and A Fowl Wish, the sweet verticality bait of Drinking and Delving, and the puzzle mod Sanity Lost.

Outside of themes, there were a number of notable entries, including the massive landscape overhauls of Waters of Morrowind and The Song of the Grazelands, the ambitious and sprawling overhaul of Tribunal's terrible dungeons with Undercity by Lord Zarcon, and entire new factions like The Arcane Watch, The Guiding Guild, and The Tri-Angled Truth.

From the delightful UI mod Inspect It to the gorgeous city expansion mod of Balmora Outskirts, the 2024 Modathon may have been a smaller event than those that had come before, but devil if it didn't still pack a punch of shiny new toys, wondrous verticality, and sky-high quality!

CHAPTER 10

X. A Triumphant OpenMW Tide (2025 and 2026)

After a slight dip in Modathon releases, 2025 would see an uptick, driven this time, not by MWSE lua, but by OpenMW lua, as the tide in the battle for gameplay supremacy well and truly began to turn, gradually, in OpenMW's favor.

A total of 179 mods were released for the 2025 Morrowind Modathon by some 108 individual mod authors, still decidedly short of the competition's height in 2022, but with a noticeable uptick in the number of mod authors participating. Indeed, the 2025 Modathon, despite ranking only 6th for total mod releases, actually ranked 2nd in terms of number of unique participants!

For a majority of those 108, some 60 in total, the 2025 Modathon was their first Modathon. A sign that a new wave of modders, a new generation, was coming into the Modathon fold, and many of them came directly from the OpenMW camp.

Prominent lua-smiths like OwnlyMe, Max Yari, Solthas, and Zerkish released a torrent of new OpenMW gameplay mods for the community. Highlights included Dynamic Sounds for OpenMW, an atmospheric and ambient dynamic sound mod, a new functional physics engine by Max Yari, an OpenMW camping and survival mod by DetailDevil, an OpenMW music management system by S3ctor, a potion-throwing mechanic by YossarianO, and to top it all off, the fully-functional and animated Barcraft by Ralts, allowing the player to finally realize their dream of becoming a traveling bard with a troupe of players!

Of course, as ever, while gameplay mods had regained their focus, courtesy of OpenMW, there were still plenty of content mods vying for the attention of quest-hungry adventurers. And to join them on their adventures, there were notably two major companion mods released for the 2025 Modathon, Caswyn - A Nightmare's Favor, featuring a very unique companion with a questline that spans into the realm of dark dreams, and Prophecy - Magicka Null, a companion mod with 7 new followers to recruit from the pages of Tamriel's past.

In the realm of dungeons, two major overhauls of the famed Vassir-Didanat Mine were released, one by Lord Zarcon, and one by SoulOEater, each promising a unique take on this quest-related vanilla location. But the biggest overhaul of all was of course Seelof, with the Vestiges of Tukushapal, overhauling and massively expanding one of the most unique tombs in the vanilla game, turning it into a true saga of vertical delights.

The year's themed challenges also brought about a number of fantastical new additions with unique quests by a variety of modders that took advantage of the themes in all sorts of different ways.

From the charming Fables & Foibles mods of Hat and Hide, The Scrib Princess, and Animal Articulation, to the pitfalls of Horror & History with Dralas Legacy and The Unseen Passages, to the temptations of Greed & Desire with a prequel to the classic Muffinwind, to the mischievous affairs of Parties & Betrayals with Seams and Schemes.

And beyond simple quests, there were new weapon-crafting mechanics, a sweet new armor set with an accompanying dungeon by Ruffin Vangarr and Katya Karrel, an entire new way to explore the world with flying ships and floating islands with Aetherwind, a new overhaul and expansion of the Odai Plateau, and far more besides.

Gameplay mods, and especially OpenMW gameplay mods, dominated 2025, but without question, there was something for everyone, a taste for every palette, it was another delicious smorgasbord of content for the Morrowind Modding Community!

2026

A new year meant a new Modathon, and in the time between 2025 and 2026, the OpenMW modding scene had only continued to grow, turbo charging the community to its highest point in 2025. That year closed with a total of over 1,900 new Morrowind mods, a high not seen since the game was first released, 23 years prior.

With Tamriel Rebuilt releasing its largest expansion of the mainland yet, a plethora of new possibilities constantly expanding on the horizon and with users downloading tens of millions of mod files for Morrowind, 2026 was set to be another big year.

And indeed, the 2026 Morrowind Modathon saw a return to the highs prior to the dip in 2023, with some 229 new mods submitted by 107 participating mod authors, a majority, some 55, joining the festivities for the very first time, many of them brand new to the community at large.

With 73 mod releases, gameplay mods were back on top of the Modathon pile, the vast majority OpenMW, but there was still a sizable contingent of MWSE lua mods making up a minority share. The biggest of them, was, without question, Inventory Extender by Ralts, featuring an overhauled inventory UI system with new features and functions.

But there were also a number of OpenMW conversions of MWSE mods, like Item Browser, Make a Profit, and Community Traits, bringing some of the as-yet not ported MWSE features to the OpenMW game engine.

Other gameplay elements were unique to OpenMW, like AI behavioral mods like Sit Down Please, or new skills like Of Pestilence and Purification, Evasion, Throwing, Staves, and Toxicology by Kildozery, all with their own unique perks. OpenMW also saw new animation mods for various player activities, like drinking and interactions, a dynamic season mod with changing seasonal shaders and foliage, new weather phenomena in the form of northern lights to dot the sky, and dynamic chatter between NPCs.

On the MWSE front, the biggest release was the addition of occlusion culling, adding a major performance upgrade to the vanilla engine, as well as dynamic NPC conversations, and the simple yet incredibly useful Load Paused, a mod that makes it so a pop-up pauses the game when you load a save, to make sure you're ready to continue.

All of these are exciting features, but it wasn't just gameplay mods, as it never is, for there is always quests, dungeons, new lands to embark upon, and companions to share your travels with, and 2026 saw plenty of these as well!

While a smaller category than past years, dungeon mods provided new overhauls for the game's tombs with OAAB - Tombs Extended and Tombs of the Zafirbel Bay, as well as a modernization and complete remaster of the Solstheim dungeon-delving mod Ayleid Remnants, utilizing the Ayleid ruin tileset of Tamriel Data to provide for atmospheric new ruins to explore.

Quests saw a delectable spread, with Death and Taxes by MollyAvast becoming the largest quest mod ever submitted for the Morrowind Modathon ,with over 100 new adventures covering both Vvardenfell and the Tamriel Rebuilt mainland, working for the game's Census & Excise Office. Master Trainers by Caeris further enhanced the game by adding quests to all of the games myriad but previously barebones master trainers, making finding them, and becoming worthy of their training, into a proper role-playing experience.

And to top it all off, Douglas Goodall was back with another quest mod, fleshing out some of the minor characters in the Guild of Mages with about a dozen new quests.

Beyond simple adventures, for those who crave someone to journey with, to experience these new lands and stories side-by-side, the 2026 Modathon had plenty to offer. From the release of Into the Void, a polished version of HangHimHigher's unfinished Julan-sized companion mod, to the Alfiq companion, a simple mage with a need to explore, to a shapeshifting bard, to a M/M romance companion inspired by the classic Morrowind mod Agony & Rapture - there was no shortage of new faces to bring on your travels!

And yet, even with all these highlights, there are still more, from new frozen landmasses, to a new set of robes and armor for the Alessian Inquisition, to overhauls of Telvanni cities, and a gorgeous expansion of Molag Mar. It may have been an event dominated by OpenMW and gameplay mods, but as always, the Modathon delivered something for everyone!

But what will it bring next? What new stories wait to be told, what new treasures to seek, what new possibilities await yet unrealized? The saga and legacy of the Modathon continue, and in 2027, it'll begin again for Morrowind's 25th anniversary. New badges will be made, new champions crowned, but who will bear the titles, who will shape the world of tomorrow? Will it be you?

The Modathon begins again on May 1st of 2027, and all who love Morrowind, all who seek to create and build upon it, are welcome to join the fun!

Long live Morrowind and long live the Morrowind Modding Community!
